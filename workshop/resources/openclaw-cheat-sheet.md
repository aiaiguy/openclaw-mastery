## What Is OpenClaw?

OpenClaw is a free, open-source AI agent that runs on your computer and connects to your messaging apps (WhatsApp, Slack, Telegram, Discord). Unlike ChatGPT or Claude, it doesn't wait for you to ask. It acts autonomously: reading your emails, scheduling meetings, browsing the web, sending messages, running tasks, and operating 24/7 in the background.

250,000+ GitHub stars. 300,000+ users. Jensen Huang called it "probably the single most important release of software... probably ever."

It is not a chatbot. It is a digital employee that never sleeps.

## Why Should You Care?

**Your people are already using it.** 22% of monitored organisations have employees running OpenClaw without IT approval. It is the fastest-growing shadow AI tool in history.

You have three choices:
1. Ban it (and lose visibility entirely)
2. Ignore it (and accept unmanaged risk)
3. **Govern it** (and capture the value safely)

## Five Things Your Board Should Do This Month

### 1. Find Out Who's Already Using It
Run network scans for WebSocket traffic on port 18789 and API calls to model providers. You will likely find instances you didn't know about.

### 2. Classify the Risk
Low-risk: personal productivity (briefings, email triage, research). No customer data.
High-risk: anything touching financial systems, customer PII, or regulated data.
Treat high-risk like privileged access. Because it is.

### 3. Set a Governance Framework
Before anyone deploys officially, you need:
- Acceptable use policy (who, what, where)
- Data classification (what the agent can and cannot access)
- Credential management (dedicated service accounts, never personal passwords)
- Incident response playbook (assume compromise, plan to rebuild)

### 4. Check Your Insurance
Ask your cyber insurer: "Does our policy cover incidents caused by autonomous AI agents?" Most policies don't address this yet. Get clarity before you need it.

### 5. Decide: Govern or Ban
If you ban it, know that 22% of organisations that tried are still finding shadow instances. If you govern it, start with low-risk use cases in isolated environments and expand with controls.

## The Honest Bottom Line

OpenClaw is powerful, cheap, and genuinely useful. It is also immature, insecure by default, and moving fast enough to break things. The right response is not fear. It is governance.

The organisations that figure out how to deploy agentic AI safely will move faster than those that ban it and faster still than those that ignore it.

The question for your board is not "should we use AI agents?"
It is "are we ready to govern them?"
